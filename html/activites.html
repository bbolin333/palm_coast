Bike Trails

Palm Coast offers over 135 miles of biking and hiking trails, weaving through scenic preserves, waterfronts, and neighborhoods. Popular routes include Graham Swamp Preserve, with technical climbs and rock gardens, and Mala Compra Trail, known for its diverse habitats and challenging terrain. The city’s year-round mild weather makes cycling a favorite pastime for both casual riders and seasoned adventurers.

Hammock Beach Resort

The Hammock Beach Golf Resort & Spa is Palm Coast’s premier oceanfront destination. Guests enjoy luxury suites and condos, two championship golf courses (including a Jack Nicklaus designed course), a full-service spa , and multiple dining options. With its pristine beach, wellness programs, and family-friendly amenities, Hammock Beach blends coastal relaxation with upscale resort living.

Washington Oaks Gardens State Park

Nestled between the Atlantic Ocean and the Matanzas River , Washington Oaks Gardens State Park is famous for its formal gardens and unique coquina rock shoreline. Visitors stroll among azaleas, camellias, and ancient live oaks, then explore tidal pools and dramatic rock formations along the beach. The park preserves 425 acres of coastal scenery, offering a tranquil mix of gardens, hammocks, and marshes. Intracoastal Waterway in Palm Coast The Intracoastal Waterway runs through Palm Coast, providing a hub for boating, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

The Intracoastal Waterway Trail

Stretching about 2–3 miles , links Waterfront Park to Hammock Dunes Bridge and offers views of dolphins, manatees, and diverse birdlife. Community events like the Holiday Boat Parade and annual Waterway Cleanup highlight its role as both a recreational and cultural centerpiece. Palm Coast really shines as a destination where nature, recreation, and luxury converge, from biking trails and gardens to waterfront adventures and resort escapes.
